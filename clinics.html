For Clinic Owners Running Meta Ads

Most owners aren't running Meta ads. They're fishing.

Make an ad. Boost it. Hope a lead bites. Next week, do it again with no memory of what worked.

The ad you can see is bad. The system you can't see isn't there at all.

Running ads and having a system are two different things. Most owners pay for the second while doing the first. Cast and pray on top. A machine that remembers underneath. That's the whole difference. Here's what it looks like in five parts.

01

The ad looks like an ad

Stock photo. Logo. "Call us today." People have trained themselves to skip ads, so that's exactly what happens to yours. Skipped before it's read.

A system opens with something worth stopping for. It earns the half second before the thumb moves on.

A real patient. A real result. A real face. Not a stethoscope on a desk.
02

No offer, no bait

"Now accepting new patients." That's a sign on a building. It tells people you exist. It gives them no reason to act today.

The offer is the ad. Everything else is decoration. One clear thing. One reason to move now.

A new patient exam, x-rays, and a cleaning for a set price. A reason to book this week.
03

The lead sits

A lead comes in at 10am. Someone gets to it tomorrow. That's casting a line and walking away from the rod. These people weren't looking for you. They forget you faster than you'd believe.

Five minutes, not five hours. Speed is the cheapest advantage you have, and almost nobody uses it. The first business to call back usually wins, before the ad ever gets the credit or the blame.

They filled out the form between things in their own day. Catch them now or lose them.
04

Budget split into nothing

Ten dollars boosted across six posts. Six thin lines in the water, none deep enough to catch anything. The algorithm never gets enough off any one of them to learn who's actually buying.

One offer, one audience, enough budget to get out of learning. One line, deep enough to catch something. Then the warm pool you already paid to build, the people who clicked and didn't book, that almost nobody goes back for.

05

No scoreboard

Ask most owners their cost per booked patient and they go quiet. They know likes. They know reach. Those don't pay anyone. Without the number, every month is a fresh guess.

The system follows the click all the way to the booked patient. If you can't name your cost per booked patient, there's nothing to fix yet. Count what shows up, not what shows off.

The owners who win on Meta aren't better at making ads. They built the system while everyone else kept fishing.
